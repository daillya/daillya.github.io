Research themes

My research at INRAE is focused on algorithms, optimisation and graph theory. One of my main points of interest is drone and robotic planning (paths, tasks) applied to the agricultural setting, taking into account multi-agents and uncertainty. My other main focus is on sensor networks in the environmental setting, mostly optimizing their deployment and data transfer (through the ConnecSenS community). I mostly focus on obtaining guarantees of quality for algorithms, on algorithmic complexity, on models and representation of constraints, etc.

I am also still working on structural and algorithmic graph theory, mostly on problems related to paths/walks, covering and metrics. Finally, I am always keen to explore various combinatorial topics (reconstruction, combinatorial games, etc).

I am a member of the informal research group AlCoLoCo, which is located in Clermont-Ferrand and works on graphs and algorithms.
